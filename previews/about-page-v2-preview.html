About

Caitlin Gardulescu

I’m someone who is deeply energized by people, creativity, travel, and great food.

I'm from Ann Arbor, Michigan, and the places I've lived have shaped me in lasting ways. I spent 10 years in Wisconsin and 9 years in New York City, and both chapters gave me a wider perspective on how people live, connect, gather, and create meaning in their lives.

Food is one of the great joys of my life, and travel feels most meaningful to me when it leads to discovery through culture, conversation, and what people share around a table. I also love meeting new people, reading, writing, making art, and paying close attention to color, mood, and the details that make something feel alive.

What Shapes Me

Food, Travel, and Curiosity

I love food more than anything in the world. It is one of the ways I feel most connected to joy, culture, memory, and other people.

When I travel, discovering new foods is one of the things I look forward to most. I’m always interested in the places that feel lived in and loved: the local restaurant someone is proud to recommend, the market stall with the perfect produce, the bakery people return to again and again, the dish that tells you something real about where you are.

  • New places come alive for me through food and atmosphere
  • I’m drawn to discovery more than checklist travel
  • The best experiences are usually the most human ones

Reading, Writing, Art, and Color

I’m an avid reader and writer, and both are central to how I think, reflect, and stay curious. I’ve always loved language, storytelling, and the feeling that a great sentence or a well-observed detail can make something instantly clearer and more alive.

I’m also a creative artist with a very visual brain. Color registers immediately for me, and I notice tone, contrast, and mood instinctively. In another life, I probably could have been a colorist.

  • I care deeply about texture, tone, and emotional resonance
  • I notice details that shape how something feels
  • Creativity is part of my everyday orientation, not a separate lane

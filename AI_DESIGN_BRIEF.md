# AI Design Brief

## Brand Direction
- Tone: serious but inviting, professional, trustworthy, and action-oriented without pressure.
- Audience: small-to-medium food, grocery, and retail companies.
- Core needs to reflect: sourcing support, streamlined operations, stronger vendor partnerships, and clear assortment strategies.

## Visual System
- Primary dark color: `#2b2b2d` for top bar, typography, and core accents.
- Secondary accent: brand gold (`#CA9C1F`) for highlights, CTAs, and emphasis states.
- Base backgrounds: clean neutrals (`#ffffff`, light warm creams) for readability.
- Typography: contemporary but professional. Prioritize clarity over decorative styles.

## UX Principles
- Communicate expertise quickly with structured sections and concise copy.
- Keep calls-to-action visible and clear, but low-pressure in wording.
- Ensure mobile-first readability and no horizontal overflow at any breakpoint.
- Favor predictable navigation and scannable content blocks.

## Motion & Live Elements
- Use subtle modern motion only: soft gradient shifts, restrained float effects, and gentle hover transitions.
- Motion must support hierarchy, not distract from content.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` by disabling non-essential animations.

## Contact Section Requirements
- Must be visually distinct and easy to find.
- Include direct email, phone, and scheduling CTA.
- Present capability fit (food/grocery/retail + operational outcomes) near contact actions.
- Keep high contrast and straightforward action paths.

## Definition of Done
- Meets color, tone, and audience constraints above.
- Passes manual checks for desktop/tablet/mobile responsiveness.
- No visual clutter, no aggressive animations, no conversion pressure language.
